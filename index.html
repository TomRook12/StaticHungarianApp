The Session Structure (every lesson)

Follow this format for all 21 lessons
▾
Listen First
3–4 min
Ask your partner to say today's phrases naturally while doing the action or pointing to the thing. You listen. Don't repeat yet. Your job is to connect sound to meaning.
Absorb the Chunks
6–8 min
Read the lesson's phrase table. Say each phrase out loud 3 times, slowly. Don't think about grammar. These are chunks — whole units you absorb like a child learns "I don't know" before knowing what "don't" means.
Use it for Real
5–8 min
Do the activity with your child. Use only the target phrases. Gesture freely. If you forget a word, gesture + say the Hungarian word you do know. Your partner praises meaning, not accuracy.
Golden rule: Your partner should never correct your grammar mid-sentence. They can model the right form by repeating it naturally ("Igen, a parkba megyünk!") — this is how children absorb corrections without shame.
Topic 1

Out & About

Five lessons covering getting out of the house, crossing roads, the playground, library, school run, and meeting people. These are your high-frequency, high-stakes moments.

Going Somewhere

A parkba megyünk.A park-ba me-dyünkWe're going to the park.
A boltba megyünk.A bolt-ba me-dyünkWe're going to the shop.
Hova megyünk?Ho-va me-dyünkWhere are we going?
Megállj!Meg-ájStop!
Várj!VárjWait!
Gyere ide!Dye-reh i-dehCome here!
Fogd a kezemet!Fogd a ke-ze-metHold my hand!
Nézz körbe!Nézz kör-behLook around! (before crossing)
Átmehetünk.Át-me-he-tünkWe can cross now.
Piros lámpa.Pi-rosh lám-paRed light.
Zöld lámpa.Zöld lám-paGreen light.
Practice Activity real life
The Announcement Game
Before you leave the house for anywhere — shop, park, school — announce it in Hungarian. Point at the door. "A boltba megyünk." Your child repeats it. When you reach the crossing say "Megállj! Nézz körbe!" and wait for them to look before saying "Átmehetünk."
Pattern notice: "-ba / -be" at the end of a place means "into/to" that place. You don't need to know the grammar rule — just notice that park → parkba, bolt → boltba. Your brain will absorb the pattern through repetition.
Advanced Sentences
Siess, menjünk, már késő van! Hurry up, let's go — it's already late!
Fogd a kezemet, mert most átmegyünk az úton. Hold my hand, because we're crossing the road now.
Megállj a sarkon, és várj meg engem! Stop at the corner and wait for me!
Nézz körbe — jön valami? Nem jön semmi. Átmehetünk. Look around — is anything coming? Nothing's coming. We can cross.
Roleplay — Leaving the House
You're about to leave for the park. Your child is dawdling. Play this out in real life as you head to the door.
You Menjünk! A parkba megyünk.Let's go! We're going to the park.
Child Hova megyünk?Where are we going?
You A parkba! Gyere ide, fogd a kezemet.To the park! Come here, hold my hand.
You Megállj! Nézz körbe. … Átmehetünk.Stop! Look around. … We can cross.

The Label Game
Pick up any toy and ask "Hogy hívják ezt?" Your child tells you the Hungarian. You repeat it. Then say "Ez egy [toy]!" together. Do 3–4 toys per session. Over a week you'll have 20+ toy words locked in — through the child's voice, not a list.

Turn Announcer

Sharing & Apologising

Mine · Yours · His · Hers · Sorry · It's okay
▾
Core Phrases
Ez az enyém.Ez oz e-nyémThis is mine.
Ez a tiéd.Ez o ti-édThis is yours.
Ez az övé.Ez oz ö-véThis is his/hers.
Osztozunk.Os-to-zunkWe're sharing.
Add ide, kérlek!Odd i-deh, kér-lekGive it to me, please!
Kölcsön adom.Köl-chön o-domI'll lend it to you.
Bocsánat.Bo-chá-notSorry.
Nem baj.Nem bayThat's okay / Never mind.
Megbocsátok.Meg-bo-chá-tokI forgive you.

Ownership words are tricky in Hungarian because they attach to the noun, but as a chunk — "Ez az enyém" (this is mine) — just learn it as one phrase first. Your child will naturally begin to extend the pattern.
Advanced Sentences
Ez az enyém, de kölcsön adom, ha kéred. This is mine, but I'll lend it to you if you ask.
Add ide szépen, kérlek — ez a tiéd lesz majd. Pass it here nicely please — then it'll be yours.
Bocsánat — nem akartam. Megbocsátasz? Sorry — I didn't mean to. Do you forgive me?
Osztozunk rajta — először te, aztán én. We're sharing it — you first, then me.
Roleplay — A Sharing Moment
One toy, two people. Play out a gentle negotiation. This works best when it's a real object both of you actually want.
Child Ez az enyém!This is mine!
You Add ide, kérlek! Kölcsön adom majd.Give it to me please! I'll give it back.
You Osztozunk. Légy kedves!We're sharing. Be kind!
Child Rendben. Bocsánat.Okay. Sorry.

Consequences & Boundaries

If/then · Stop · Not allowed · Calm down
▾
Core Phrases
Elég!El-égEnough! / That's enough.
Abbahagyod!Ab-bo-ho-dyodStop that! / Cut it out.
Ez nem szabad.Ez nem so-bodThat's not allowed.
Ha ezt csinálod, akkor...Ho ezt chi-ná-lod, ok-korIf you do that, then...
Tedd vissza!Tedd vis-soPut it back!
Nyugodj le!Nyu-godj lehCalm down!
Lélegezz!Lé-le-gezzBreathe!
Gyere, megbeszéljük.Dye-reh, meg-be-sél-yükCome, let's talk about it.
Értem.Ér-temI understand.
Rendben.Rend-benOkay. / Alright.
Key Insight child development
Why this matters linguistically
Consequence language is emotionally loaded — which means it gets remembered fast. "Elég!" said firmly once in context will stick in your memory better than 20 vocabulary drills. Use it authentically in real moments. Your child will also pick up on your consistency.
Advanced Sentences
Ha ezt még egyszer csinálod, akkor elmegyünk haza. If you do that one more time, we're going home.
Elég! Tedd vissza, és gyere ide! Enough! Put it back and come here!
Nyugodj le — lélegezz, aztán megbeszéljük. Calm down — breathe, then we'll talk about it.
Értem, hogy mérges vagy — de ez nem szabad. I understand that you're angry — but that's not allowed.
Roleplay — Setting a Limit
Practise this calmly before you need it in a real moment. Say the lines out loud — tone matters as much as words.
You Abbahagyod! Ez nem szabad.Stop that! That's not allowed.
You Nyugodj le. Lélegezz.Calm down. Breathe.
Child Rendben.Okay.
You Gyere, megbeszéljük. Értem.Come, let's talk about it. I understand.

Mealtime Script

Practice Activity the full ritual
Mealtime Ritual
Build a consistent mealtime ritual entirely in Hungarian: call everyone with "Gyertek enni!", say "Jó étvágyat!" before eating, ask "Kérsz még?" mid-meal, and end with "Köszönöm az ételt." Three fixed phrases at the start, middle, and end. Ritual repetition is the most powerful memory tool there is.
After 4–6 weeks of consistent use, your child will prompt you if you forget to say something. That's the moment you know it's working — you've built a family language habit, not just a lesson.
Advanced Sentences
Gyertek enni — az étel az asztalon van! Come and eat — the food's on the table!
Ülj le és egyél lassan — nem kell sietni. Sit down and eat slowly — there's no rush.
Megettél mindent? Szuper! Köszönöm az ételt. Did you eat everything? Great! Thank you for the food.
Felállhatok? — Persze, de előbb igyál vizet! May I get up? — Of course, but drink some water first!

Run through the complete mealtime in Hungarian from call to finish. Do this every day until it's automatic.

Pattern notice: You'll see lots of words ending in -t here (nadrágot, zoknit, pólót). In Hungarian, the direct object of a verb gets a -t ending. You don't need to know the rule — just notice that when you're telling someone to put something on, it sounds like the word has a little "t" on the end.
